Bonnet Springs Park

4.9★ (2687 reviews)

This sprawling 180-acre preserve in Lakeland transforms team building into environmental stewardship, combining hiking trails through native Florida ecosystems with hands-on conservation projects. Groups can tackle trail maintenance, native plant restoration, or invasive species removal while building authentic connections through shared purpose. The park's diverse habitats—from wetlands to pine flatwoods—create a dynamic outdoor classroom where teams learn about local ecology while working toward tangible conservation goals.

What to Expect

Teams arrive for a brief orientation about the park's conservation mission before dividing into work groups with specific environmental tasks. You might spend the morning removing invasive plants along the boardwalk, followed by native seed planting in restoration areas, then wrap up with a guided nature walk to see the impact of previous volunteer efforts. Projects are designed to accommodate different fitness levels while ensuring everyone contributes meaningfully to the park's ecosystem health.

Who It's For

Ideal for environmentally conscious organizations with 10-50 participants who want their team building to create lasting impact. The combination of physical work and environmental education resonates especially well with corporate sustainability teams, nonprofits, and groups seeking alternatives to traditional indoor activities.
